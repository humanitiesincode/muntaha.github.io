Core Themes

Literature from Jharkhand often revolves around Jal, Jungle, Zameen (Water, Forest, Land), resistance against displacement, and the preservation of tribal identity.

  • Ancestral Knowledge: Preservation of myths and totems.
  • Resistance: Voices against cultural and geographical dispossession.
  • Nature: Deep spiritual connection to the environment.
Prominent Voices
Ram Dayal Munda: Intellectual leader; author of Adivasi Astitva aur Jharkhand Asmita.
Nirmala Putul: Renowned Santhali poet writing on the struggles of tribal women.
Jacinta Kerketta: Contemporary voice focusing on 'Jal, Jungle, Zameen' and social justice.
Anuj Lugun: Award-winning poet known for Bagh aur Sugna Munda ki Beti.
Linguistic Heritage

Jharkhand's literature is rich in diverse languages including Santhali, Mundari, Ho, Kurukh, and Kharia. Significant milestones include the creation of the Ol Chiki script by Pandit Raghunath Murmu for the Santhali language.
